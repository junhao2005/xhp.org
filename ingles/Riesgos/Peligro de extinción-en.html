Polar Bears

The loss of their habitat, sea ice, due to climate change, is the greatest threat to the survival of polar bears. However, they also face other threats such as conflicts with humans, unsustainable hunting and impacts generated by industry.

Panda bears

This peaceful, bamboo-loving, gentle-looking animal faces multiple threats. Deforestation and habitat fragmentation: Its forest habitat, in the mountainous regions of southwest China, has been fragmented, forcing the isolation and reduction of panda populations.

Iberian Lynx

Every year in Spain, about 100 lynxes die from being run over and are victims of environmental crimes such as poaching and the use of poisons or illegal traps. This problem is smaller in number of victims, but equally avoidable: deaths by shooting, snares or snares represent an irreparable loss of lynxes every year.

The Whales

The threats to the species are numerous: humans through hunting, fishing nets and industry (collisions with ships, pollution and oil and gas development) and climate change threaten the lives of these animals.
